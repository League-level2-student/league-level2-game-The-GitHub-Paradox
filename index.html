Meme Soundboard

by: Eric Li

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I decided to make this game because I was inspired by someone on one of my discord servers asking others what a certain meme song was. That sparked an idea, and I made a soundboard dedicated to catching people up the the latest meme music.

2. What I Learned

I learned that a flashy soundboard would lead to more interactivity between user and game. I also learned to change the pages and overlay of certain sounds to prevent the windows from crashing the computer.

3. Architecture of My Game

I have 4 different components of the finished game. The audio tester, and 3 soundboard pages. If you play a song on one soundboard, and click another song, then the sound that corresponds to the most recent button clicked would override the song that came before.

4. Technical Implementation

There are 3 interconnected soundboards and an audio tester. I am proud about the range of buttons and letters that could be clearly seen in a 500 x 500 screen. I am also proud of the sounds that I chose, as most of them were suggested to me by my sister.

5. Most Challenging Part

A challenging part of this project was to get the final .jar to run with sound. Originally, it would just be the buttons and frames, but I did some further studying, and overcame that obstacle by placing both the .jar and audio files in the same folder.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

I would probably add more pages and a less complicated weave of view that isn't so much "random" as it is now. I would also change the project to have a welcome and instructions screen.
